    Introduction

    Welcome to A Journal of Management, the official Management Journal of the Pune Institute of Business Management (PIBM). Our journal aims to disseminate cutting-edge research and insightful contributions in the field of management. This editorial policy outlines the guiding principles and standards that govern the publication process, ensuring the quality and credibility of the content we publish.

    Scope and Focus

    A Journal of Management covers a broad spectrum of management topics, including but not limited to strategic management, organizational behavior, human resource management, marketing, finance, operations management, entrepreneurship, leadership, and innovation. We invite submissions of original research papers, empirical studies, case studies, literature reviews, and conceptual articles that advance the understanding and practice of management.

    Editorial Independence

    As the official journal of PIBM, A Journal of Management operates with complete editorial independence. The decision to accept or reject a manuscript is based solely on its scholarly merit, relevance to the field, and adherence to our editorial guidelines. The editorial team is committed to maintaining an unbiased and impartial review process.

    Peer Review Process

    All submissions to A Journal of Management undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review process. This process involves the evaluation of manuscripts by experts in the respective domains, ensuring the highest quality and credibility of published articles. Authors and reviewers remain anonymous during the review process to maintain objectivity.

    Plagiarism and Ethical Considerations

    At A Journal of Management, we uphold the highest ethical standards in publishing. Plagiarism, data fabrication, and any form of academic misconduct are strictly prohibited. Authors are responsible for ensuring the originality of their work and must appropriately cite all sources. Submissions must adhere to the ethical guidelines set forth by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE).

    Authorship and Contribution

    All authors listed on a manuscript must have made significant contributions to the research presented. The corresponding author is responsible for obtaining consent from all co-authors and ensuring their approval of the final version of the article before submission.

    Conflicts of Interest

    Authors are required to disclose any potential conflicts of interest that could influence the research or its interpretation. Such conflicts may include financial interests, affiliations, or personal relationships that could be perceived as biasing the research.

    Data and Reproducibility

    A Journal of Management encourages authors to share the data underlying their research whenever possible. Transparent reporting enhances the credibility of the research and allows for verification and reproducibility of the results.

    Copyright and Licensing

    Authors retain the copyright to their work published in A Journal of Management, granting us a license to publish the article. Readers are granted the right to use, share, and adapt the content for non-commercial purposes, provided proper attribution is given to the original work.

    Corrections and Retractions

    In the event of errors or inaccuracies discovered in published articles, A Journal of Management will take appropriate measures to correct or retract the content promptly. We prioritize academic integrity and are committed to rectifying any potential issues swiftly.

    Editorial Discretion

    The editorial team at A Journal of Management reserves the right to make editorial decisions, including revisions and abridgments, to enhance the clarity and readability of published articles while preserving the integrity of the author's message.

    Open Access Policy

    A Journal of Management follows an open-access publishing model, ensuring that all content is freely accessible to readers worldwide. We believe in the value of knowledge sharing and promoting the widespread dissemination of research.

    Manuscript Submission Guidelines

    Authors must adhere to the submission guidelines provided by A Journal of Management. Failure to comply with the guidelines may result in delays or rejection of the submission.

    Contact Information

    For any inquiries or clarifications regarding our editorial policy or the submission process, please contact the editorial team.

    Note: The above editorial policy serves as a general guideline for A Journal of Management at PIBM and can be adapted as necessary to suit the journal's specific requirements and practices.
